## 025.0 — Purpose

The Page Architecture defines how the **finished manuscript** becomes its **canonical printed form**.

This is not typesetting — it is **composition**. Every page becomes part of the **performance**. The page itself carries meaning. *(CF-CON-525 · CF-CON-067 Artifact not novel)*

*(Aligns: CF-CON-111 Two-phase production · CF-CON-112 Composer's Pass · BUILD-024 performance model)*

---

## 025.1 — The First Principle

**The story is written first. The page is composed second.**

Never reverse this order. **Narrative truth always precedes visual elegance.** *(CF-CON-526 · CF-CON-028 Story wins over cleverness)*

Page design must **never dictate** the story during composition. Page architecture applies **after** the manuscript is complete.

---

## 025.2 — The Page as a Breath

Every page is **one complete breath** — not necessarily one idea · not necessarily one scene.

Readers **inhale** with the opening · **exhale** with the turn. The page becomes **physiological**. *(CF-CON-527 · CF-CON-074 Breathing as instrument · BUILD-024)*

---

## 025.3 — The Page Turn

A page turn is a **narrative event**. It can:

| Function | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Reveal** | New information at the fold |
| **Delay** | Withhold until turn |
| **Interrupt** | Break expectation |
| **Echo** | Mirror prior turn |
| **Suspend** | Hold tension across the gap |
| **Release** | Exhale · resolution |

Every page turn deserves **intentional placement**. *(CF-CON-528 · expands CF-CON-114 Page turn tool)*

---

## 025.4 — The Visual Density Map

Every page receives a **density profile**:

| Density | Character |
|---|---|
| **Dense** | Accelerated eye · compressed tempo |
| **Balanced** | Default reading comfort |
| **Open** | Room to breathe |
| **Sparse** | Weight on each word |
| **Silent** | White space dominant |

The **sequence** of densities creates visual rhythm across the entire novella. *(CF-CON-529 · CF-CON-101 Narrative Meter · BUILD-024)*

---

## 025.5 — The Canonical Grid

Only after the manuscript is **stable**:

- Trim size · margins · line length
- Font size · leading · widows and orphans · paragraph spacing

These decisions are **aesthetic and narrative**. The canonical edition **preserves** them. *(CF-CON-530 · CF-CON-504 One typeface · BUILD-023)*

---

## 025.6 — The Architecture of White Space

White space is not unused paper — it is **silence made visible** *(BUILD-022)*.

Large white spaces should be **rare**. Each corresponds to a meaningful pause in the **emotional score**. *(CF-CON-531 · CF-CON-457 Language of Silence)*

---

## 025.7 — The One-Word Page

Perhaps **only once**. Perhaps **never**.

If a single word occupies an entire page — it must justify that decision for **every future reader**.

**Spectacle is forbidden. Necessity is required.** *(CF-CON-532 · CF-CON-491 Law of typographic rarity)*

---

## 025.8 — The Opening Page

The first page **teaches** the reader how to read the book.

Visual rhythm establishes **trust** — nothing unusual · nothing distracting. The architecture begins **invisibly**. *(CF-CON-533 · BUILD-011 Covenant · CF-CON-490 Default typography sacred)*

---

## 025.9 — The Final Page

The last printed page leaves room for **silence** — not because words ran out · because the story **intentionally hands the experience back to the reader**.

The page participates in the **ending**. *(CF-CON-534 · CF-CON-471 Final silence · CF-CON-522 Final tempo)*

---

## 025.10 — The Spread

Two facing pages form **one visual composition** — not merely two independent pages.

Some moments require deliberate **balance** across the spread · others intentional **asymmetry**. *(CF-CON-535)*

---

## 025.11 — The Eye Path

Eyes travel **before** minds interpret. The canonical edition considers:

- Line breaks · paragraph placement · negative space · visual weight

**Without drawing attention to itself.** *(CF-CON-536 · BUILD-010 Five Instruments · CF-CON-495 Typographic rhythm)*

---

Every page receives an **architectural profile** — part of the Resonance Matrix. *(CF-CON-537 · expands CF-CON-347 Page Score)*

---

## 025.13 — The Material Book

The **physical object** matters — paper · texture · opacity · weight.

The reader's **hands** become part of the experience. The book is not a delivery system — it is the **instrument**. *(CF-CON-538 · CF-CON-067 · CF-CON-068 Form supports experience)*

---

## 025.14 — The Digital Edition

Digital editions may preserve: **text · rhythm · typography**.

They **cannot perfectly reproduce** canonical page architecture.

| Edition | Status |
|---|---|
| **Printed** | Definitive performance score *(CF-CON-523)* |
| **Digital** | Faithful adaptation — **not replacement** |

*(CF-CON-539)*

---

## 025.15 — The Rule of Invisibility

Readers should **almost never** think about page architecture while reading.

Notice constantly → **decoration**. Feel effects without noticing → **succeeding**. *(CF-CON-540 · CF-CON-524 Invisible conductor · CF-CON-488 Live with this)*

---

## 025.16 — The Final Architecture Test

Before approving the canonical edition:

1. Does every page **deserve** to exist exactly as composed?
2. Does every page turn carry **intention**?
3. Does every silence **breathe**?
4. Does every spread support the **emotional score**?
5. Would changing the layout **weaken** the experience?

If not → **continue composing**. *(CF-CON-541 · CF-CON-501 Print test typography · CF-CON-226 Conductor's Test)*
