## 002.0 — Purpose

Every page must produce **discovery**.

Not information. Not explanation. **Discovery.**

Discovery is never given. Discovery is **earned**.

The reader should feel they have **found** something. Not been **told** something.

---

## 002.1 — The Prime Directive

This is the governing **engineering rule**:

> **Every page must increase discovery without decreasing clarity.**

Everything must pass this test:

Story · Dialogue · Typography · Rhythm · Silence · Character · Mystery · Emotion · **Everything.**

*(Merges BUILD-000.002 wonder/clarity with discovery — both required.)*

---

## 002.2 — The Discovery Pyramid

Discovery operates in five layers. *(CF-CON-176)*

| Layer | Name | Reader experience |
|---|---|---|
| **One** | Story Discovery | "What happens next?" · Adventure · Conflict · Momentum |
| **Two** | Character Discovery | "I understand this person differently." · Relationships · Motivations · Empathy |
| **Three** | Reality Discovery | "I misunderstood how this world works." · Time · Memory · Identity · History |
| **Four** | Self Discovery | "I made assumptions." · Reader becomes aware of themselves |
| **Five** | Human Discovery | "I should approach people differently." · **The novel leaves the page** |

*(Aligns: BUILD-001 §001.4 · Five Locks · BUILD-005 Reader Arc)*

---

## 002.3 — The Rule of Curiosity

Readers should always possess: *(CF-CON-177)*

- **One** answered question
- **Two** active questions
- **One** hidden question

Every answer opens another door. **Curiosity never stops.**

---

## 002.4 — Discovery Is Not Confusion

**Immutable.** *(CF-CON-178)*

| Confusion | Discovery |
|---|---|
| Reduces curiosity | Increases curiosity |
| "I have no idea." | "I almost understand." |

---

## 002.5 — The Goldilocks Principle

Every scene exists between three failures: *(CF-CON-179)*

```txt
Too obvious
     ↓
  Correct        ← creates wonder
     ↓
Too obscure
```

---

## 002.6 — The Three Discoveries Per Scene

Every scene must contain at least **three** discoveries: *(CF-CON-180)*

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Immediate** | The protagonist escapes |
| **Delayed** | Someone lied |
| **Invisible** | A recurring symbol changes meaning |

---

## 002.7 — The Reader Participation Model

Readers should never receive complete information.

Readers **assemble reality** — exactly as human beings do.

*(Aligns: BUILD-010 Physics of Reading · BUILD-001 §001.13 Absence)*

---

## 002.8 — The Discovery Loop

Every discovery follows this rhythm: *(CF-CON-181)*

```txt
Observation → Interpretation → Assumption → Evidence → Revision → Understanding
```

The novel repeats this cycle **continuously**.

---

## 002.9 — The Curiosity Engine

Questions become fuel. Readers constantly ask: Who? Where? Why? When? How?

But the novel quietly wants them asking:

> **"What assumption caused my first answer?"**

*(Aligns: BUILD-008 Assumption Engine · BUILD-001 §001.13)*

---

## 002.10 — The Assumption Ladder

Readers naturally build ladders. *(CF-CON-182)*

```txt
Unknown person → Must be important → Must be hero → Must be trustworthy → Must be correct
```

The novel should **never build these ladders**.

Readers build them. The novel later **examines** them.

*(Aligns: BUILD-008 · BUILD-001 §001.3)*

---

## 002.11 — The Information Economy

Never answer immediately. Never delay unnecessarily.

Information possesses **value**. Spend it wisely.

*(CF-CON-183)*

---

## 002.12 — Questions Have Lifecycles

Every question receives classification: *(CF-CON-184)*

| Class | Example |
|---|---|
| **Immediate** | "What is that object?" |
| **Medium** | "What happened here?" |
| **Long** | "What broke time?" |
| **Entire Novel** | "What does it mean to understand another human?" |

---

## 002.13 — Discovery Through Contrast

Readers discover through comparison: *(CF-CON-185)*

```txt
First encounter → Second encounter → Recognition
```

Everything important appears **more than once**. Never identically.

---

## 002.14 — Discovery Through Repetition

Repeated things become meaningful — not because repeated, because **transformed**.

**Bridge example:**

| Read | Bridge means |
|---|---|
| First | Bridge |
| Second | Escape |
| Third | Connection |
| Fourth | Memory |
| Fifth | Identity |

**Bridge never changes. Meaning does.**

*(Aligns: Resonance CF-CON-094 · Echo Ledger · BUILD-003 Pillar Three)*

---

## 002.15 — The Discovery Map

Every discovery belongs somewhere: *(CF-CON-186)*

Story · Character · World · Memory · Emotion · Rhythm · Typography · Silence · Humanity · Reader

**Nothing exists outside these domains.**

---

## 002.16 — The Discovery Tempo

Discovery has rhythm — like music:

```txt
Fast discovery → Slow discovery → Rest → Fast discovery
```

Readers **breathe** through discovery.

*(Aligns: BUILD-009 Music Theory · BUILD-006 Emotional Score)*

---

## 002.17 — The Discovery Density

Every movement has different density:

| Movement | Density |
|---|---|
| One | Many discoveries · Small |
| Two | Fewer · Larger |
| Three | Deep |
| Four | Transformative |
| Five | Architectural |

---

## 002.18 — The Discovery Horizon

Readers should always sense there is **more**.

Like standing on a hill. The horizon never disappears. It merely **moves**.

---

## 002.19 — Discovery Through Silence

Sometimes the greatest discovery is created by **omission**.

Nothing explained. Nothing highlighted. Readers notice. Later — meaning arrives.

*(Aligns: BUILD-004 Law Six · Silence Bible)*

---

## 002.20 — Discovery Through Rhythm

```txt
Rhythm → Prediction → Expectation → Surprise → Attention → Discovery
```

**Music becomes cognition.**

*(Aligns: BUILD-009 · RHYTHM_BIBLE)*

---

## 002.21 — Discovery Through Empathy

Readers discover **people**. Not facts.

Facts support people. People never support facts.

*(Aligns: BUILD-001 §001.1 · BUILD-007)*

---

## 002.22 — The Prime Question

Every page must answer:

> **"What is the reader discovering?"**

If the answer is **Nothing** — **delete the page.**

*(CF-CON-187)*

---

## 002.23 — Discovery Validation

Every scene passes five checks:

- ✓ Story Discovery
- ✓ Character Discovery
- ✓ Emotional Discovery
- ✓ Reader Discovery
- ✓ Hidden Discovery

---

## 002.24 — Failure Conditions

Discovery fails when:

- Explanation replaces observation
- Confusion replaces curiosity
- Lecture replaces experience
- Coincidence replaces inevitability
- Architecture overshadows humanity
- Reader admiration replaces reader transformation

*(Aligns: BUILD-001 §001.6 · BUILD-004 · BUILD-013)*

---

## 002.25 — The Deepest Directive

The novel never rewards readers for being **clever**.

It rewards them for becoming **patient**.

*(CF-CON-188)*

```txt
Patience → Discovery → Humility → Empathy → fulfills BUILD-001
```

---

## 002.26 — Traceability

| BUILD | Role |
|---|---|
| **001** | Purpose — **Transform the reader** |
| **002** | Mechanism — **Discovery** |

Everything that follows — rhythm, silence, typography, chronology, music, symbolism, page turns — must answer:

> **Does this help the reader discover, or does it merely tell?**

If it merely tells, it doesn't belong in Chronoforge.

---

## Closing — The true engine

The reader changes not because of the ending, the twist, or time travel.

**It happens because the book repeatedly rewards the reader for replacing certainty with curiosity.**

If curiosity feels **emotionally rewarding** — not just intellectually satisfying — Chronoforge reaches beyond mystery mechanics.

*(CF-CON-188 · Aligns: Antagonist Certainty CF-CON-003 · BUILD-001 §001.4)*
