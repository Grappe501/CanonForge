> **"Every civilization is a story people tell together about how to live."**

> We're not inventing countries. We're inventing **ways of being human**.

**Renamed from roadmap "Civilizations"** — government · economy · military are **downstream**. Civilizations begin with one shared answer to one shared question.

---

## 027.0 — Purpose

The Civilization Engine defines how societies **emerge · grow · evolve · fracture · renew**.

A civilization is **not a government** — it is a **shared way of making meaning**. Everything else grows from that. *(CF-CON-565 · CF-CON-566 · CF-CON-584 Downstream institutions)*

*(Aligns: BUILD-026 Civilization Test · CF-CON-557 · BUILD-007 Human Experience)*

---

## 027.1 — The First Law

Every civilization attempts to **solve a problem** — not all the same problem.

| Orientation | Example drive |
|---|---|
| Stability · freedom · memory | Discovery · beauty · harmony |

**None entirely right · none entirely wrong.** Every civilization is **incomplete**. *(CF-CON-567 · CF-CON-380 Incomplete consciousness · CF-CON-560 World limits)*

---

## 027.2 — The Founding Question

Before creating any society:

> **What question shaped these people more than any other?**

Examples: *How do we survive? · belong? · remember? · remain free? · live with loss? · pass wisdom?*

**Everything grows from that seed.** *(CF-CON-568 · expands CF-CON-557 Civilization test)*

---

## 027.3 — The Six Foundations

Every civilization rests on **six living foundations**. *(CF-CON-569)*

| Foundation | Governs |
|---|---|
| **Memory** | How past is preserved — books · songs · architecture · elders · **silence** |
| **Relationship** | Strangers to neighbors · repair · trust |
| **Work** | Why people labor — necessity · purpose · honor · service · discovery |
| **Learning** | Knowledge transfer — schools · apprenticeship · ritual · exploration |
| **Beauty** | What is protected when impractical — reveals **values** |
| **Hope** | What future is believed possible — **direction of history** |

*(Integrates: BUILD-019 memory · BUILD-021 symbols · BUILD-022 silence · BUILD-024 tempo)*

---

## 027.4 — The Rule of Imperfection

No civilization **entirely admirable** · no civilization **entirely corrupt**.

Every society: **strengths · blind spots · unintended consequences · moral complexity**.

Readers should **understand before judging**. *(CF-CON-570 · CF-CON-164 Assumption Engine)*

---

## 027.5 — The Civilization Rhythm

Every society has its **own tempo** — rush · wait · celebrate · reflect.

Readers recognize a civilization by **rhythm before politics**. *(CF-CON-571 · BUILD-024 · CF-CON-553 World music)*

---

## 027.6 — The Language of a People

Language shapes thought — not merely vocabulary:

Metaphors · questions · greetings · blessings · farewells · **silences**.

Each civilization reveals itself through **how it speaks**. *(CF-CON-572 · BUILD-022 · CF-CON-457)*

---

## 027.7 — The Invisible Rules

Every culture has rules outsiders cannot immediately see — **not laws · assumptions**.

Readers discover gradually. **Greatest cultural differences are often invisible.** *(CF-CON-573 · CF-CON-164 · BUILD-008)*

---

## 027.8 — The Shared Stories

Every civilization tells stories about itself — accurate · myth · aspiration · warning.

**All influence behavior.** *(CF-CON-574 · BUILD-020 motifs · CF-CON-431)*

---

## 027.9 — The Civilization Memory

Societies remember **selectively** — celebrate · forget · regret · refuse to discuss.

**Collective memory becomes another character.** *(CF-CON-575 · CF-CON-552 World memory · BUILD-019)*

---

## 027.10 — The Edge of the Map

Every civilization imagines what lies **beyond itself** — curiosity · fear · longing.

Those assumptions reveal **more than geography**. *(CF-CON-576 · CF-CON-554 Horizon principle)*

---

## 027.11 — The Measure of Greatness

**Not** measured by wealth · armies · monuments.

**Measured by:**

- How it treats the **vulnerable**
- How it handles **disagreement**
- How it **teaches children**
- How it **remembers mistakes**
- How it **welcomes the unfamiliar**

*(CF-CON-577 · BUILD-004 Law · BUILD-011 Covenant)*

## 027.12 — The Collision of Worlds

When civilizations meet — conflict **not inevitable** · harmony **not guaranteed**.

Deepest encounters: two ways of understanding humanity **recognize truths in one another**.

**That is where Chronoforge lives.** *(CF-CON-578 · CF-CON-549 Gravitational center · CF-CON-548)*

## 027.14 — The Human Test

Every civilization must allow:

> **"I would want to learn from them."**

Even if the reader would **not** want to live there. Every culture possesses **wisdom worth carrying home**. *(CF-CON-580 · CF-CON-171 Experience not sermon)*

## Human race as discovery *(CF-ENTRY-066 · CF-CON-582 · CF-CON-583)*

> **"We are all equal... we belong to the human race."**

Chronoforge must **not prove** this — stories rarely change people by proving propositions.

Five civilizations disagree on history · technology · art · memory · even **time** — yet the reader recognizes something deeper than difference.

Not because the book **tells** them — because each civilization **loves · grieves · hopes · fears · remembers · wonders** in unmistakably human ways.

> **"The human race" won't be a slogan. It will become a discovery.**

*(CF-CON-582 Human race discovery · CF-CON-583 Experience not proof · CF-CON-145 Article XVII · BUILD-007 Humanity)*

Discoveries stay with people **longer than arguments**.
