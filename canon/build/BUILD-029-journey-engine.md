## 029.0 — Purpose

The Journey Engine governs every transition between **places · ideas · relationships · states of consciousness**.

Journeys are **not interruptions between scenes** — they are where **transformation begins**. Movement itself becomes **narrative**. *(CF-CON-605 · CF-CON-606)*

*(Aligns: BUILD-028 Movement-family tools · CF-CON-589 · genre pillars journey into another world)*

---

## 029.1 — The First Law

Every journey answers **three questions**:

1. **Where** is the traveler going?
2. **Why** are they going?
3. **Who will they become** because of it?

If the third answer is *"no one changes"* → **reconsider the journey**. *(CF-CON-607 · CF-CON-623 What journey changes)*

---

## 029.2 — The Five Journeys

Every meaningful journey belongs primarily to one category. *(CF-CON-608)*

| Journey | Domain |
|---|---|
| **Physical** | Movement through space — classic adventure |
| **Temporal** | Experienced time — past · present · future · memory · expectation |
| **Relational** | Distance between consciousnesses changes — sometimes without moving |
| **Intellectual** | Understanding expands · assumptions collapse · patterns emerge |
| **Inner** | Identity · hope · purpose — **deepest journey** |

*(Integrates: BUILD-009 · BUILD-016 · BUILD-018 · BUILD-005 Reader Arc)*

---

## 029.3 — The Threshold

Every journey begins crossing a **threshold** — door · bridge · question · goodbye · decision.

Thresholds divide **who we were** from **who we are becoming**. *(CF-CON-609 · CF-CON-528 Page turn · BUILD-005)*

---

## 029.4 — The Road

The middle is **never empty** — conversations · observations · doubt · wonder.

Travel is **never** merely waiting for the next destination. *(CF-CON-610 · BUILD-024 tempo on the road)*

---

## 029.5 — The Arrival

Arrival is **not** the end — it is the beginning of a **new relationship**.

The destination changes because someone **arrived** · the traveler changes because they **stayed**. *(CF-CON-611)*

---

## 029.6 — The Return

Returning is **never** the reverse of leaving.

**Home remains · the traveler does not.** Return reveals true **cost and value**. *(CF-CON-612 · CF-CON-618 Reader journey · reread = return home)*

---

## 029.7 — The Map

Maps describe **geography** · journeys describe **transformation**.

Chronoforge values **journeys over maps**. Readers remember **experience · not mileage**. *(CF-CON-613 · CF-CON-558 Rule of world discovery)*

---

## 029.8 — The Vehicle

Vehicles matter **only because they shape experience**.

Walking ≠ flying · crowded vessel ≠ solitary travel. **Method of movement is part of the story.** *(CF-CON-614 · BUILD-028 Movement family · CF-CON-588 Cost of tools)*

---

## 029.9 — The Companions

Travel together → rarely unchanged.

Shared movement → shared memory → relationship → **identity reshaped**. *(CF-CON-615 · BUILD-007 Human Experience · CF-CON-578 Collision of worlds)*

---

## 029.10 — The Cost of Distance

Every journey leaves something behind: **comfort · certainty · time · safety · assumptions**.

**Distance always has a price.** *(CF-CON-616 · BUILD-028 Cost of tools · transportation cost)*

---

## 029.11 — The Horizon

The horizon is **possibility** — not merely geography.

Another horizon always exists. **Wonder survives** because the world cannot be exhausted. *(CF-CON-617 · CF-CON-554 Horizon principle)*

---
