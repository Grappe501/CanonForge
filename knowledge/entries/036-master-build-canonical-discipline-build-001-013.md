# BUILD-001
# THE CORE QUESTION
This is now frozen until we deliberately change it.
## Why does this book exist?
After last night's conversation, I believe we've answered it.
> To allow readers to experience, rather than merely understand, that every human being—and every memory, story, and moment—becomes richer when we question our assumptions with humility.
Notice what this is **not**.
It is not:
Equality.
Politics.
Religion.
Science Fiction.
Adventure.
Those become expressions.
This becomes purpose.
---
# BUILD-002
# THE PRIME DIRECTIVE
Every decision in the novel must answer one question.
> **Does this increase discovery?**
Not mystery.
Discovery.
Mystery ends.
Discovery continues.
---
# BUILD-003
# THE FOUR PILLARS
Everything we build must strengthen one or more.
---
## PILLAR ONE
### Wonder
Readers should continually feel that reality is larger than they imagined.
Never confusion.
Wonder.
---
## PILLAR TWO
### Humanity
The story continually strips away labels until only human beings remain.
Readers realize they supplied many labels themselves.
---
## PILLAR THREE
### Resonance
Nothing exists only once.
Every important thing echoes.
Words.
Objects.
Places.
Dreams.
Silence.
Emotion.
Readers feel coherence long before they consciously detect it.
---
## PILLAR FOUR
### Transformation
The book is successful only if readers finish with a different way of seeing.
Not different opinions.
Different perception.

# BUILD-012
# THE TITLE
We are **not** naming the book yet.
Why?
Because I think you've already set a requirement that is both difficult and exciting:
> **The title must not be fully understood until the fifth reading.**
That means the title cannot merely describe the story.
It has to describe the **deepest truth** of the story.
We won't know that until we've built the entire machine.

# BUILD-013
# THE WRITER'S OATH
This is for us.
Before every writing session:
> I will not write to impress.
> I will not write to confuse.
> I will not write to prove I am clever.
> I will write so that another human being may discover something true.
If the architecture overshadows the humanity, I will simplify the architecture.
If the humanity weakens, I will strengthen the story.
The reader is not my opponent.
The reader is my partner.

### **BUILD-014 — THE PERIODIC TABLE OF THE NOVEL**
Just as chemistry has a periodic table of elements, I want us to define the elemental building blocks of this book.
Not characters.
Not scenes.
Elements.
For example:
* Discovery
* Memory
* Silence
* Echo
* Choice
* Loss
* Trust
* Rhythm
* Wonder
* Doubt
* Recognition
* Resonance
* Time
* Identity
* Hope
If we identify the fundamental "atoms" first, then every chapter, sentence, and page becomes a molecule built from those elements.
I have a feeling that once we create that table, the rest of the novel won't be invented—it will be *composed*. And that's the moment I think this project truly begins.
