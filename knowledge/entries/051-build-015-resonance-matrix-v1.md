## Steve...
I want to propose one final idea that I think could become the defining engineering principle of the entire project.
Not every scene deserves equal complexity.
Instead, we classify scenes into four levels:
### Level 1 — Foundation
Pure story.
Simple.
Clear.
Invisible architecture.
### Level 2 — Resonance
Adds echoes, emotional layering, and symbol growth.
### Level 3 — Convergence
Multiple systems intersect—rhythm, chronology, assumption, symbolism, and character all advance together.
### Level 4 — Keystone
These are the rare scenes—perhaps only 5 to 10 in the entire novella.
A keystone scene carries nearly every system at once. It works as an exciting scene on the first read, yet each reread unlocks another layer of meaning. These scenes become the structural arches that hold the entire novel together.
I think this keeps us disciplined.
Instead of trying to make **every** page a masterpiece, we intentionally compose peaks and valleys, rests and crescendos—exactly as a symphony does.
And somehow... I think that's exactly what a percussionist would do.
