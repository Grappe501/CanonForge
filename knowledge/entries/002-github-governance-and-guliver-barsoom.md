Guliver of Mars and A Princess of Mars are two foundational works of the "planetary romance" genre. While they share many similarities, Burroughs expanded and refined many of the ideas Arnold introduced.
## Guliver of Mars (1905)
### Plot
Guliver Jones, a U.S. Navy lieutenant, acquires a magical flying carpet that transports him to Mars. There he encounters rival kingdoms, strange creatures, political intrigue, and a beautiful princess. He becomes entangled in a struggle to save her people while searching for a way to prove himself in an alien world.
### Themes
* Adventure and exploration
* Heroism through perseverance
* Love and devotion
* Honor and duty
* Idealism confronting political reality
### Setting
Mars is portrayed as an ancient, fading civilization with:
* Vast deserts
* Ancient cities
* Rival kingdoms
* Exotic wildlife
* Medieval-style cultures mixed with advanced wonders
### Summary
A naval officer is magically transported to Mars, where he fights for justice, falls in love, and becomes part of a civilization very different from Earth's.

### Plot
John Carter, a former Confederate cavalry captain, mysteriously finds himself transported to Mars (called **Barsoom**). Because Mars has weaker gravity, he possesses extraordinary strength and agility compared to its inhabitants.
He is captured by the towering Green Martians before meeting the beautiful Dejah Thoris, a princess of the Red Martians. Carter becomes a warrior, diplomat, and leader while attempting to prevent war and rescue Dejah Thoris.
### Themes
* Heroism
* Honor and personal integrity
* Romance
* Civilization versus barbarism
* Loyalty and friendship
* Self-determination
### Setting
Barsoom is richly imagined with:
* Dry seas and dead ocean beds
* Air plants sustaining the atmosphere
* Flying battleships
* Eight-legged riding beasts
* Distinct races (Green, Red, White, Black, and Yellow Martians)
* Ancient cities and advanced-but-aging technology
### Summary
John Carter becomes one of Mars's greatest heroes after arriving from Earth, using his superior strength and unwavering sense of honor to unite warring peoples and save the woman he loves.

## Similarities
Both novels feature:
* An American hero transported to Mars.
* A romance with a Martian princess.
* Warring Martian civilizations.
* Ancient, declining cultures.
* A journey from outsider to respected hero.
* A blend of adventure, romance, and fantasy.
## Differences
**Guliver of Mars**
* Reads more like a fairy tale.
* Magic (the flying carpet) is central.
* Focuses on atmosphere and romance.
* Less detailed world-building.
**A Princess of Mars**
* Uses pseudo-scientific explanations.
* Emphasizes action and combat.
* Develops a complex Martian history, geography, cultures, and technology.
* Introduces a memorable cast and a long-running series.
## Influence
Many literary historians believe *Guliver of Mars* influenced *A Princess of Mars*. Both feature an American soldier transported to Mars who falls in love with a Martian princess and becomes involved in local conflicts. However, there is no definitive evidence that Edgar Rice Burroughs directly borrowed from Edwin Lester Arnold. Burroughs never publicly acknowledged the earlier novel as an inspiration, though the similarities have been noted by critics for decades.
Today, *A Princess of Mars* is generally regarded as the more influential work because of its expansive world-building, memorable characters, and lasting impact on science fiction and fantasy. It inspired countless later creators, including those behind modern space-opera adventures such as *Star Wars*, while *Guliver of Mars* remains an important but lesser-known precursor.
