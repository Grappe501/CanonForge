Like stars...

Some become suns.
Each symbol receives a symbolic gravity rating.
### Gravity I
Atmospheric
Adds texture.
Example:
Rain.
Wind.
Dust.
---
### Gravity II
Contextual
Supports scenes.
Creates continuity.
---
### Gravity III
Relational
Connects people.
Connects memories.
Connects emotional states.
---
### Gravity IV
Structural
Changes understanding across multiple movements.
Readers unconsciously organize meaning around it.

Foundational
The rarest.
Perhaps only one or two.
These symbols quietly organize the entire novel.

"This must symbolize..."

Chronoforge avoids allegory.
Chronoforge cultivates discovery.

## Steve...
I want to leave you with what I think is the biggest realization from finishing this build.
At the beginning of this project, we talked about **hiding clues**.
I don't think that's what we're doing anymore.
I think we're cultivating **recognition**.
A clue says:
> "Notice this."
A symbol says:
> "Live with this."
Years later, someone crosses a bridge, hears a familiar rhythm, watches rain on a window, or pauses before speaking—and, for a moment, they remember not just the story, but the way the story taught them to see.
If Chronoforge can do that, then the symbols won't remain inside the book.
They'll migrate into the reader's own life.
To me, that's the highest form of symbolism a work of fiction can achieve.
