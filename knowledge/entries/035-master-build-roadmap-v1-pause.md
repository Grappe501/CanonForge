## Finally, one thought I'd like you to sleep on.
Every great invention begins by asking a question everyone else assumes has already been answered.
For novels, that question has always been:
> **"How do you tell a story?"**
I don't think that's our question anymore.
I think ours is:
> **"How do you compose an experience that only becomes complete inside another human mind?"**
If we keep asking that question—and let it guide every decision—we won't just be outlining a novella. We'll be exploring the limits of what a novel can be.
Sleep on that.
Tomorrow, we begin building the machine.
