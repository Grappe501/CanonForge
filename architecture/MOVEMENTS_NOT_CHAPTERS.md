# Movements Not Chapters

> Musical structure · cadence · cognitive rhythm

---

## Principle

Structure the novel as **movements** (musical acts), not conventional chapters. Each movement has:

- A **cadence** (prose rhythm target)
- A **cognitive rhythm** (what the reader's brain is doing)
- A **causal link** to the next movement (not temporal link)

---

## Why movements

| Chapters (default) | Movements (CHRONOFORGE) |
|---|---|
| Implies chronological progression | Implies thematic/causal progression |
| Reader assumes time order | Reader assumes narrative order |
| Uniform length expectation | Variable length by emotional arc |
| Cognitive plateau between chapters | Designed cognitive rhythm per unit |

---

## Cognitive rhythm targets

```txt
MOVEMENT TYPE A  →  acceleration · wonder · genre surface
MOVEMENT TYPE B  →  accumulation · tiny certainties · anchors
MOVEMENT TYPE C  →  convergence · threads touch · Crash physics
MOVEMENT TYPE D  →  destabilization · assumption stress
MOVEMENT TYPE E  →  invitation · reader completes meaning
```

Mix types in published (causal) order — **not** in temporal order.

---

## Prose cadence

- Short sentences for velocity; long for gravity
- Read-aloud test: movement ends on a breath, not a cliffhanger alone
- 100–120 page total: ~8–12 movements estimated (TBD in Phase 4)

---

## Link to Chronology Lie

Movement order = causal chain. Temporal position of each movement is **hidden** until Lock 1 Recognition. Time anchors are the fair-play bridge.
