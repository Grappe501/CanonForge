# Five Locks

> Assumption keys · Recognition language · visibility doctrine

---

## Model

Each read provides an **assumption key** that unlocks a Recognition layer:

```txt
READ 1  →  adventure surface · default assumptions
READ 2  →  Lock 1 · chronology Recognition
READ 3  →  Lock 2 · character Recognition
READ 4  →  Lock 3 · world Recognition
READ 5  →  Lock 4 · story Recognition
READ 6+ →  Lock 5 · reader Recognition (sixth protagonist)
```

Exact read-count mapping may compress; **five Recognition moments** is the invariant.

---

## Lock definitions

| Lock | Assumption broken | Reader says |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Chapters are chronological | "The timeline wasn't what I thought." |
| **2** | Characters are who they appear | "These characters aren't who I assumed." |
| **3** | World rules are as presented | "The world works differently than I believed." |
| **4** | Genre/story type is fixed | "The story I was reading wasn't the real story." |
| **5** | Reader is external observer | "I was the missing piece all along." |

---

## Recognition vs twist

| Twist | Recognition |
|---|---|
| New information retroactively inserted | Same facts · new meaning |
| Reader feels tricked | Reader thinks "how did I not see that?" |
| Violates Golden Rule | Passes fair-play audit |

Benchmark: *The Sixth Sense* — one pass. CHRONOFORGE — five passes.

---

## Visibility vs importance

On Read 1, the reader accumulates **hundreds of tiny certainties** — each fact true, each meaning shallow. On later reads, the same certainties become **evidence**.

```txt
VISIBLE  ≠  IMPORTANT
IMPORTANT ≠  VISIBLE (until Recognition)
```

---

## Time anchors

Per-movement fair-play micro-clues. Present on Read 1; inert until Lock 1 Recognition.
